Study 1¶

The semiconductor sample is $h_{\mathrm{s}} = 100 \: \mathrm{nm}$ thick, with dielectric constant $\epsilon_{\mathrm{s}} = 3.0 - 0.10 j$ and charge mobility $\mu = 10^{-10} \: \mathrm{m}^2 \mathrm{V}^{-1} \mathrm{s}^{-1} = 10^{-4} \: \mathrm{cm}^2 \mathrm{V}^{-1} \mathrm{s}^{-1}$. To mimic a metal, the substrate's dielectric constant is set to $10^{6}$.

The cantilever has a resonance frequency of 62 kHz, a voltage of 3 V, and an artificially large radius of 150 nm. It is positioned 40 + 150 = 190 nm above the surface.

Vary the semiconductor's charge density and plot the dissipation.

sample1_jit = SampleModel1Jit(

    h_s=100E-9,
    mu=1.0E-10,
    rho=1e21,

    z_r=190E-9)

Having set the charge density, vary the tip-sample separation. Set cantilever location to $h + R$ because the cantilever charge is located a distance $R$ away from the leading edge of the cantilever's tip.

In [9]:
h = ureg.Quantity(np.logspace(start=np.log10(400), stop=np.log10(40), num=41), 'nm')

result2 = {
    'h': h,
    'gammaV': ureg.Quantity(np.zeros_like(h), '(pN s)/(V^2 m)')
}

R = sample1_jit.cantilever.R
for index, h_ in enumerate(h):

    sample1_jit.cantilever.d =  R + h_.to('m').magnitude
    sample1_jit.z_r = R + h_.to('m').magnitude
    norm = 1/ureg.Quantity(sample1_jit.cantilever.V_ts, 'V')**2
    result2['gammaV'][index] = (norm * gamma_perpendicular_jit(theta1norm_jit, sample1_jit)).to('(pN s)/(V^2 m)')
In [10]:

plt.ylabel('friction $\gamma^V_{\perp}$ [(pN s)/(V$^2$ m)]')
